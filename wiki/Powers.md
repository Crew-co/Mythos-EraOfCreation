# How the age is played

Nothing in the Age of Chaos is typed. Every beat is an act you perform in the world; the engine's
trigger, ritual and director services turn those acts into the turning of the story.

| Who | Act | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Gaia** | Right-click the earth with the **Seed of Earth** | Bears the next Titan from the front of the spirit queue — a child rises where you struck, inside a ring of moss. |
| **Uranus** | **Right-click one of his children** | The ground opens and takes them to Tartarus. Do it once and Gaia feels it. |
| **Gaia** | Raise the **grey adamant**, then kneel over it | The forge-rite. Two acts, and the Adamantine Sickle is made. |
| **A child of Gaia** | **Strike Uranus with the sickle** | The one blow that can unmake the Sky. Nothing else can — punch him all you like. |
| **Nyx** | **Sneak** | Draws the night over herself and vanishes for thirty seconds. |
| **Chaos** | **Right-click a mortal** | Opens the gap beneath them and lets them fall out of the story. The old and the divine are too solid to unmake. |

## When the house is thin

Every required beat has a Director fallback. If nobody claimed Uranus, or there aren't enough spirits
to be born by hand, the age doesn't hang — after a grace window the Director narrates the shortfall and
strikes the beat itself, and the world turns to the Titanomachy anyway. You can watch the whole first
age with two players and an empty queue, and it still ends the way it should.
